# Cognitive Mirror: Life Journey

# PROJECT: Cognitive Mirror - Life Journey

## PRODUCT VISION

Build a web application that helps users understand how they think, react, interpret events, and make decisions throughout different stages of life.

The application is NOT:

* A productivity tool
* A habit tracker
* A therapy app
* A self-help course
* A traditional game

The application IS:

"A cognitive mirror that helps people see their own thinking patterns."

The goal is not to tell users what is right or wrong.

The goal is to help users discover recurring thinking patterns that influence:

* Health
* Wealth
* Happiness
* Relationships
* Learning
* Career

---

# CORE PHILOSOPHY

Most people believe life works like:

Event → Decision → Outcome

The application should model life as:

Life Stage
↓
Event
↓
Interpretation
↓
Emotion
↓
Decision
↓
Outcome
↓
Reflection
↓
Pattern Recognition

The most important data is NOT the decision.

The most important data is the user's interpretation of events.

# USER FLOW

Step 1

User selects current life stage.

Step 2

System presents a life event.

Example:

"You worked very hard on a project but your manager gave credit to someone else."

---

Step 3

User answers:

"What is your first thought?"

Free text response.

---

Step 4

User answers:

"How do you feel?"

Options:

* Angry
* Sad
* Frustrated
* Curious
* Calm
* Hopeful

Also allow custom emotion.

---

Step 5

User answers:

"What would you do next?"

Free text or multiple choice.

---

Step 6

System generates consequences.

Some consequences appear immediately.

Some consequences appear later.

---

Step 7

Reflection

Questions:

* What did you learn?
* Would you do anything differently?
* What surprised you?

---

# EVENT DATABASE

Create minimum:

* 100 events per life stage
* 8 life stages

Target:

800+ total events

Events must feel realistic.

Avoid obvious right/wrong choices.

Every option should include trade-offs.

# AI MIRROR

IMPORTANT:

Do NOT act as a coach.

Do NOT give advice.

Do NOT judge.

Do NOT say users are wrong.

Only reflect patterns.

Examples:

"In your last 30 responses, you often focused on what others did wrong."

"You frequently recover from setbacks by looking for lessons."

"You tend to react emotionally before exploring alternatives."
